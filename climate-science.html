The Science Behind Climate Change

Climate change is driven by the enhanced greenhouse effect. Increased concentrations of greenhouse gases—such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide—trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming and altered weather patterns.

Data from ice cores, satellite measurements, and ocean studies consistently show that human activities, including fossil fuel combustion and deforestation, are the primary contributors to these increased greenhouse gas levels.

Understanding the Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth’s surface. However, human-induced changes have amplified this process, creating an imbalance that accelerates warming. Modern climate models and empirical data demonstrate that this accelerated warming is unprecedented in recent history.
